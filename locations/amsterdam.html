01 / The Amsterdam thesis

Amsterdam is Europe's quietest AI-search laboratory.

Amsterdam is where half of Europe's US-originated SaaS runs its European operations — Booking, Adyen, Uber, Netflix, Tesla, Stripe, Snowflake, Atlassian and hundreds more. It's also where a generation of Dutch scale-ups — Mollie, Bunq, Picnic, Mews, Messagebird — compete in categories that are, without exception, researched by buyers in AI assistants before a vendor meeting is ever booked.

Two things make this market unusual. First, the English-first working language means Amsterdam content ranks and gets cited across the entire Anglophone EU without translation. Second, the Dutch pragmatism toward technology means most in-house teams are already experimenting with LLM workflows — they're ready for a GEO conversation that their continental peers are still three quarters away from.

The result: Amsterdam is one of the highest-leverage cities in Europe for GEO right now. The work compounds across markets the client never paid to enter.
