01 / What GEO is

Rankings are the shallow end. AI citations are the ocean.

Generative Engine Optimization is the practice of making a brand the kind of entity an LLM wants to cite. When a user asks ChatGPT "what's the best X in Europe" or Perplexity "who are the leaders in Y," the engine synthesizes an answer from a handful of sources. Your brand is either one of those sources — or it isn't.

GEO optimizes for the citation, not the click. The KPIs are citation frequency, answer inclusion rate, entity strength and share of AI voice. The techniques overlap with classical SEO (schema, crawlability, content structure) but the targets are different, and the work is deeper.

Most agencies are still selling keyword sheets. We're engineering the knowledge graph entries your brand shows up in.

02 / Why now

Your competitors aren't winning AI search. They're just already inside it.

Google AI Overviews now appears on an overwhelming majority of commercial search queries. ChatGPT has hundreds of millions of weekly users, many of whom no longer touch a classical search engine. Perplexity is becoming the default research tool for technical buyers. Gemini is baked into Workspace.

The brands that get cited today are writing the reference layer for the next decade of AI answers. Every citation reinforces entity strength. Every entity reinforcement increases the probability of the next citation. It is a compounding position, and it closes.

Waiting until AI search "matures" is like waiting until Google "matures" in 2003.

03 / Our GEO Framework

The 5-layer GEO stack.

Every engagement works through these five layers in order. Skipping layers is why most "GEO" attempts fail.

01.

Entity Audit

We map how each major LLM currently perceives your brand. What does ChatGPT "know" about you? What does Perplexity cite when someone asks about your category? Where are the gaps in your Wikidata / knowledge-graph entries? This produces the baseline every subsequent layer is measured against.

02.

Structured Data & Schema

We deploy a full schema architecture — Organization, Service, Product, Person, Article, FAQPage, HowTo, with explicit @id anchoring and sameAs disambiguation across Wikidata, LinkedIn, Crunchbase and authoritative external sources. This is the machine-readable spine every LLM uses to decide who you are.

03.

Content Architecture

We rebuild content around entities and question clusters, not keyword lists. Definitive pillar pages. FAQ layers that match how users actually ask LLMs. Glossaries that anchor key terms. Internal linking that forms a semantic network instead of a flat sitemap.

04.

Citation Distribution

LLMs cite what other credible sources cite. We engineer third-party entity mentions — research publications, podcasts, data repositories, directory listings, authoritative editorial placements — that reinforce the entity signals without looking like link-building (because they aren't).

05.

Measurement & Iteration

We track citation frequency across 12+ engines, answer-inclusion rate for target prompts, share of AI voice against competitors, and entity-strength deltas over time. Dashboards update weekly. Tactics iterate monthly. Nothing is set-and-forget.
